About Me

Asmita Jana

Email: asmitajana@gmail.com

I am a computational materials scientist who builds predictive models for materials, interfaces, and processes relevant to sustainable energy. I use Density Functional Theory, Molecular Dynamics, and Machine Learning to connect structure → mechanism → performance, and I develop workflows that scale from single calculations to high-throughput studies

At Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, I investigate nanoconfined catalysis– how they are created, why they are stable, and how they enhance catalytic activity and selectivity. Part of it is highlighted in Elements. I also develop and benchmark MLIP-driven workflows for surface chemistry, including evaluations of FAIR Chemistry's UMA models on open catalysis and materials datasets to quantify accuracy, robustness, and practical tradeoffs for adsorption and reaction modeling.

During my PhD at Massachusetts Institute of Technology, I modeled gas and water transport through graphene-based membranes. I also built a reactive MD workflow to simulate carbon fiber manufacturing from low-cost pitch precursors, by modeling crosslinking reactions in heterogeneous organic molecules. My simulations reproduced key mechanical properties and revealed a new pathway towards affordable, high-performance carbon fibers, work that got featured in MIT News.
